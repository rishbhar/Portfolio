Online Forums Redesign

2 weeks

Tags

Interaction Design

Many popular internet communication services have evolved over the past decade to meet new needs. Emails, social networking websites etc have undergone major evolution for their interfaces, usage, and also for how we feel about them. The landscape is changing with current users who are tech savvy, demand high quality and well designed products. However, there is one web service which has survived without having to undergo a big change: online forums. For this project, we had to decide on which community of users we would be redesigning the forums for. We talked to our users to bring in the insights. We focussed on COMPUTER IMGINATION, one of the 7 themes of good design for attempting this redesign. We also learned how use our resources effectively to form a good argument for what we choose to do.
What are the problems with online forums?

Currently online forums do not tackle a few things affectively:

Catching up - It is not an easy task for a new user to catch up with an ongoing conversation, specially if there is a large number of participants.

Unaesthetic Design - The look and feel for most of these online forums have stayed the same for a long time. It is the same plain, thread based design which can span across multiple pages for long conversations.

Cognitive Processing - Users tend to engage in short conversations. The overwhelming text based design does scare some users away. For example, some people would not even try using reddit even though it is a good product. So we had an idea about the problems, the next step was to choose user group.



Letting the creative juices flow to explore how forums could be!

My team and I were excited to try innovative non-tradiotional ideas for the redesign. So to challenge current designs of many forums ,approach to information discovery and lack of motivation for users (in our case, travellers), we came up with some of these sketches which were generative, and sparked conversation about the possibilities.

Doing research and narrowing down on hikers/backpackers

We chose to design for travel forums users. Travel forums have always seen a good number of users looking not only for simple answers, but sometimes also for descriptions on travel experiences with rich media. We looked into TripAdvisor, LonelyPlanet, TravBuddy and some subreddits: /r/travel, /r/backpacking, /r/campingandhiking etc.

We formed a survey for these travellers, and posted it on these websites/forums and blogs:
1) What got you into this (forum)?
2) What keeps you coming back to (forum)?
3) What do you value about the (forum) community?
4) What would the perfect forum for you be or look like?

For the moderators:
1) Why did you want to become a moderator of (forum)?
2) Why did you become a moderator of multiple subreddits?

 While we were getting to know more about our users, we were sketching too.

Insights - Like minded users connect and help each other

From the responses that we got from the online survey, and by talking to some backpackers/hikers about how they travel, we got to know about a few things.

1) Travelers are eager to read the stories of others so that they can learn from them and improve their own travel experiences.
2) In turn, travelers also enjoy sharing their own experiences in order to help others.
3) Travel forum frequenters enjoy the community that develops when they connect with people who share their interests.

We considered the current problems with the design as per the prompt (because they do exist for users too!). And through our research, sketch work and converstions with some of these users, came our design core.

Testing, validating and iterating

Out of many forums users, we decided on travellers to re-design for. I felt if computer imaginative ideas had to be tried, we could design for travellers. We talked to our users, tested our ideas, got their feedback, and iterated on a few elements of our design.







We took the feedback from the users, and iterated on a few elements on the design with 10 more ideas. Some of which have been shown below.











What I learned

1) The seemingly coolest of all the ideas might not even contribute to the right design.
2) Computer Imagination does not necessarily mean bold, fancy or futuristic ideas. Learning how resources can be used in the best way possible can be also pretty computer imaginative.
